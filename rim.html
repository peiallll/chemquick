*abundancies must add to 100, as they are percentages.

Relative Atomic Mass:

Previous:

The Relative Atomic Mass of an element is found by the sum of the products of the masses of each of its Isotopes by their respective abundancies in percentages. Then, divide that value by 100. For example, Chlorine has 2 isotopes, Cl35 and Cl37. Cl35 has 75% abundance, and Cl37 has 25% abundance. We do 35x75 + 37x25. Divide it by 100. That gives us the relative atomic mass of Chlorine, which is 35.5.
